# First TikTok Video — "What's Your Brain Score?"

## Video Concept: The Brain Score Challenge

**Format:** POV / Challenge video
**Length:** 45–60 seconds
**Goal:** Go viral by making people curious about their own Brain Score, drive App Store downloads
**Vibe:** Casual, authentic, slightly competitive — NOT a polished ad

---

## The Script

### HOOK (0–3 seconds)
**[Text on screen: "I built an app that tells you how smart you are"]**

> *You, talking directly to camera:*
> "I built an app that gives you a Brain Score out of 1000... here's what I got."

### THE DEMO (3–25 seconds)
**[Screen recording of you taking the Brain Score Assessment in Memori]**

Show quick clips of each part of the 3-minute assessment:
1. **Digit Span** — numbers flashing, you tapping them back
2. **Reaction Time** — screen turning green, you tapping fast
3. **Visual Memory** — grid tiles lighting up, you recalling the pattern

> *Voiceover while showing gameplay:*
> "It tests your memory, reaction time, and visual processing in 3 minutes."

**[Keep clips fast-paced — 2-3 seconds each, show yourself getting some right and some wrong to keep it real]**

### THE REVEAL (25–35 seconds)
**[Show the Brain Score result screen with your score, Brain Age, and Brain Type]**

> *Back on camera, genuine reaction:*
> "I got a [YOUR SCORE]... Brain Age [YOUR AGE]... that's [react honestly — hype it up or roast yourself]"

**[Text on screen: "Brain Age: XX" and "Top XX%"]**

### THE CTA (35–45 seconds)
**[Back on camera]**

> "It's called Memori — link in bio. Drop your Brain Score in the comments, I wanna see if anyone can beat me."

**[Text on screen: "What's YOUR Brain Score? 🧠"]**

## Production Notes

### What You Need
- iPhone with Memori installed
- Screen recording turned on (Settings > Control Center > Screen Recording)
- Good lighting for face-cam clips (natural light or ring light)
- Quiet space for voiceover

### Recording Steps
1. **Film the hook** — look into camera, say the opening line, keep energy up
2. **Screen record the full Brain Score assessment** — do it for real, don't fake it
3. **Film your reaction** to the result — be genuine, people can tell when it's forced
4. **Film the CTA** — casual, like you're telling a friend
5. **Edit in CapCut** — stitch it all together

### Editing Tips (CapCut)
- Use quick cuts (no clip longer than 3-4 seconds)
- Add text overlays for key moments (score, brain age, percentile)
- Use a trending sound at low volume underneath your voiceover
- Add subtle zoom-ins on the score reveal for drama
- Speed up the gameplay clips slightly (1.2–1.5x)

## Why This Format Works

1. **Curiosity gap** — "how smart you are" makes people want to know THEIR score
2. **Challenge format** — "drop your score in comments" creates engagement
3. **Authentic** — you built it, you're showing it, it's not an ad
4. **Shareable** — people will screen record their own scores and duet/stitch
5. **Brain Score is inherently viral** — everyone wants to know their number (like IQ tests, personality quizzes, etc.)

## Key Principles for Your TikTok Strategy

1. **Post from a personal account, not a brand account** — TikTok suppresses brand content. "Solo dev who built an app" performs 10x better than "Download our app"
2. **First 1-2 seconds are everything** — if they don't stop scrolling, nothing else matters
3. **The Brain Score IS your viral mechanic** — every video should tie back to "what's your score?"
4. **Consistency > perfection** — post daily for 30 days. Your first video probably won't blow up. Your 15th might
5. **Engage in comments** — reply to every comment for the first hour, the algorithm treats comments as the #1 signal

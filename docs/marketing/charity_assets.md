### `getmemoriapp.com/giving`

# 10% of profit goes back. You vote on half.

We're not a charity. We're a subscription app. But Memo exists to fight the engagement-engineered platforms that have been rewiring your attention for the last decade. So 10% of every dollar of Memo's profit goes back to that fight.

We split it: **5% to Center for Humane Technology — every single quarter.** **5% to a cause you vote on.** Real receipts, public ledger, every 90 days.

---

## Why CHT gets the fixed half

Center for Humane Technology is the org founded by **Tristan Harris** — the ex-Google design ethicist whose work became *The Social Dilemma*. They run the policy, research, and education side of the same fight Memo runs on the consumer side.

Funding them is the highest-leverage non-Memo dollar against the actual adversaries. So they're locked in for half. Forever.

[Visit humanetech.com →]

## Why you pick the other half

Half is locked. Half is yours. Every quarter, we put 3–5 vetted causes on the shortlist — digital wellness, mental health, kids' online rights, education, attention research, anti-attention-economy advocacy. You vote in TikTok comments / Instagram polls / our app survey. Winner gets 5% of that quarter's profit.

We curate the shortlist (no scams, no partisan causes, no random GoFundMes). You decide.

## How "profit" is defined (the honest math)

So the donation is real, not theoretical:

- **Net revenue** (subscription revenue minus Apple's 15% cut)
- **Minus direct app costs** (servers, RevenueCat fees, third-party services)
- **NOT minus** founder salary or marketing — those are reinvestment, not cost-of-goods

10% of what's left, every quarter, gets split 5/5.

In growth quarters the donation goes up. In slow quarters it goes down. We're not promising 10% of revenue (kills early-stage businesses). We're promising 10% of the actual money the business made.

---

## How voting works

- **Vote opens:** week 1 of each quarter — shortlist of 3–5 vetted causes drops on TikTok + here
- **Voting period:** 8 weeks
- **Vote closes:** week 8 — winner announced, donation processes by week 12
- **Eligibility to vote:** open to everyone — no subscription required
- **Vote channel:** TikTok comments are primary, with cross-post to Instagram. We tally manually each quarter for now (small enough audience). When this gets too big we'll add an in-app survey.
- **Founder veto:** retained for vote-brigading or controversial outcomes. If used, publicly explained on this page in the relevant quarter's row.

---

## Why we're doing this

In honest order:

1. **It's right.** If you're going to spend years building a product that fights the attention economy, fund the people fighting it at the policy level too.
2. **It keeps us honest.** A public commitment with a public ledger is harder to abandon than a private intention.
3. **It separates us from the competition.** Brick takes your money. Opal takes your money. Forest takes your money. We take your money and give 10% of profit back — half to CHT, half to a cause you pick.

## 7. Brand voice notes for charity copy

When writing about the donation in any context:

DO:
- Frame as "fighting back" not "giving back"
- Keep specific numbers (5/5 split, quarterly, $X this quarter)
- Acknowledge it's not pure altruism
- Punch at competitors who don't do this ("Brick takes your money. We give 10% of profit back, and you pick half.")
- Lead with WHY (the fight), then WHAT (the donation + vote)
- The vote IS the brand differentiator — emphasize it, not the dollar amount

DON'T:
- Use "philanthropy", "give back", "social impact", "mission-driven" — wellness/corporate vocab
- Compare us to Patagonia / TOMS / etc. unless we ARE going to formalize via a 1% for the Planet–style program (we aren't yet)
- Imply users should subscribe FOR the charity — they should subscribe because the app works
- Inflate the donation impact — keep it sized correctly. Real org, real receipts, real change but proportional.
- Cite specific CHT programs we haven't verified are still active. Use general descriptions ("policy work", "education", "research") unless you know the specifics.
- Frame the community vote as a "feature" — it's a structural commitment, not a gimmick

## 8. Where in the v2.0 launch sequence this gets wired

- **Pre-launch (this week):** confirm CHT donation page accepts donations the way you'd expect, draft Q1 shortlist (4 candidates), confirm /giving page builds in time
- **At v2.0 ship:** paywall footer line live, App Store description includes closing paragraph, /giving page goes live with empty ledger + "Q1 vote opens [date]"
- **Week 1 post-launch:** Beat 1 video (shortlist drop) + open Q1 vote
- **Week 4-8 post-launch:** Beat 2 (vote update) + Beat 3 (winner announce)
- **Week 12 (~Q2 start):** Beat 4 (receipt drop) + Q2 shortlist drops

The 4-beat cadence becomes the recurring marketing rhythm forever. Schedule it on your calendar at quarter start. If a beat slips, post a make-good — the audience cares about consistency more than the specific dates.
